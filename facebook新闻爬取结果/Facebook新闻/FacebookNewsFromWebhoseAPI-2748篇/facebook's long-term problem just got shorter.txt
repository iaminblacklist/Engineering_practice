By Jennifer Saba
NEW YORK, Nov 1 (Reuters Breakingviews) - Mark Zuckerberg vowed to do more to combat fake content in response to the fallout from Russia-backed ads. He warned profitability will take a hit just as the social network added almost $5 bln to the bottom line. As government scrutiny grows, the $500 bln firm has little choice.

CONTEXT NEWS
- Facebook on Nov. 1 reported that third-quarter revenue rose 47 percent year-over-year to $10.3 billion. Net income increased 79 percent to $4.7 billion, or $1.59 per share. The figures exceeded analysts' consensus expectations for revenue of $9.8 billion and earnings of $1.28 per share, according to Thomson Reuters data.
- Chief Executive Mark Zuckerberg said the company planned to double the number of staff working on safety and security in the next year, to 20,000, and that such actions will hit profitability.
- "We are serious about preventing abuse on our platforms," he said in a statement. "We are investing so much in security that it will impact our profitability."
