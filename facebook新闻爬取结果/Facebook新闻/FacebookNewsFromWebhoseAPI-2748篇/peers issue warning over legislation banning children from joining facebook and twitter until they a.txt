C hildren will be banned from joining Facebook and Twitter until they are 13, under legislation to be debated by peers this week.
The Government's Data Protection Bill will legally enshrine the age at which children are allowed to create accounts online.
However ministers are working to head off a possible defeat in the Lords by cross-party peers who insist the measure must be accompanied by new rules forcing companies to adapt their sites for younger users.
The move comes as the Home Secretary is to separately urge executives from internet giants to "go further and faster" in their efforts to tackle child sexual exploitation, during a visit the US this week.
Amber Rudd is expected to warn firms that technology has made "vile child sexual abuse content vastly easier to find". It is their "moral duty" to "help us turn the tide on this horrendous scourge", she will say.
